The Bakwena also known as Bakoena are one of the most tswana speaking tribes in Botswana. They are located in Molepolole, they originate from South Africa and they migrated to Botswana.

These people are led by a Kgosi(Chief) and his name is Kgari Sechele III. His role is to lead the clan by maintaining order, carry the traditions(setso), belief(tumelo) and culture(Ngwao) of the clan.

The totem also known as The SERETO of the Bakwena symbolizes or represents unity, and the Bakwena people are not allowed to harm the crocodile(kwena), and or eat the animal.

Bakwena people value respect(Botho) and cultural presevation, these tradtions are passed down from generation to generation through mainane(storytelling), elders telling their children proverbs(diane) which help the childern to know whats wrong and right.
